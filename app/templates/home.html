What you'll see

Each line is shown cropped from the original page scan. The text outside the detected line boundary is dimmed so you can focus on the target line, while still seeing enough context to understand what surrounds it.

Example: a cropped line with surrounding context dimmed

Step 1 Validate

You see a line image and its OCR transcription. Read the transcription and decide whether it matches what you see in the image. Each line will be shown to up to 2 annotators independently, enabling inter-annotator agreement.

✓ Validate V

The OCR text is correct. Confirms the line and removes it from your queue. Once 2 annotators validate it, the line is fully resolved.

✎ Skip to edit E

The text needs correction. Flags the line and takes you directly to the review editor (Step 2) for this line.

→ Skip S

Come back later. The line stays in your queue and will reappear in a future session.

✕ Unsure / not qualified U

You are not confident about this line (unusual script, damaged text, language you don't read). Hides it permanently from your queue without counting as an annotation — other annotators can still reach it.

Step 2 Review & Correct

Lines that were flagged in Step 1 (or that have never been validated) appear here. The transcription is now editable — type your correction directly in the text box.

✎ Save edit Enter

You changed the text and want to save your correction. The button activates automatically once you start typing.

✓ Validate as-is V

The OCR text is actually correct after all — no change needed. Validates without saving any corrected text.

⏭ Skip S

Not sure right now. If you typed something, your partial edit is saved and pre-filled next time this line appears.

✕ Skip forever X

Permanently hides this line from your queue. Other annotators can still see and correct it.

Timer & ready prompt

Before each line is revealed, a short prompt asks "Are you ready?" — click the button or press Ctrl+Enter to start. This lets us measure how long each annotation takes without counting time spent away from the screen. The timer runs until you submit an action.
